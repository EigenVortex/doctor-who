Example Social Network

Below is an interactive social network constructed from the book Downtime by Marc Platt. Within the graph, the search tools in the window on the left and the scaling tools at the bottom can help you find your way around quickly. Mousing over a node will reveal the character it represents. To move the graph, click and drag it. To view a character up close, click their node — this will display their full name, modularity, in-degree, out-degree, and eigenvector centrality. It also displays a clickable list of their connections to other characters.





Sources of Error

Below each visualization, there will be a segment addressing modifications made to the dataset that may have offset the true number of interactions that occur between characters in the story.

In Downtime, for example, all instances of "Edward" in the interaction dataset were replaced with the ID "Travers". While "Edward" could refer to either Edward Travers or Edward Waterfield, Edward Waterfield is deceased throughout the duration of the story and does not directly interact with others. Edward Travers, on the other hand, is an entity who interacts directly with other characters in the story. Without a careful manual scan of the text to verify which "Edward" was being referred to, the margin of error introduced to the social network by this generalization was deemed negligible.


Troubleshooting

If you encounter a problem with the graph display, there will be a link below the visualization to view the network in a new tab. If you cannot view the graph at all, please take a look at the EigenVortex GitHub page, where you can download the visualization files and host the interactive tool locally. If you have display issues with multiple graphs, please consider sending an email to eigenvortex@gmail.com to alert us to the problem.
