The Big Idea

Sarah Jane Smith made her Doctor Who debut in 1974 as the Doctor’s new companion. Her consistent appearances in Doctor Who television serials, print media, radio broadcasts, and other audio adventures amassed her a substantial number of in-universe social connections. In the early 2000s her own television series, The Sarah Jane Adventures, spawned a unique web of print and audio stories. The Sarah Jane Adventures and Doctor Who were intricately connected during this period, with both titular characters making guest appearances on each others’ programs. This project explores the evolution of Sarah Jane’s social networks across these varied media over the course of her appearances in the Whoniverse.

In order to accomplish this, interactions from television episodes and books featuring Sarah Jane are being quantified. For the time being, TV interactions are coded manually while textual interactions are analyzed with natural language processing tools in Python. The end result is an interactive social network for each story (i.e. each television serial and each book plot). Eventually, these microcosmic networks will be conglomerated into a large network showcasing all of Sarah Jane's connections at once.
